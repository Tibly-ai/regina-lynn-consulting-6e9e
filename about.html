About

Twenty years on
the sales floor.

Before the workshops and the keynotes, there was a quota, a whiteboard, and a lot of tough conversations. That's still where the work starts.

I started in retail sales at nineteen, moved into admissions for a private career college, and spent the better part of a decade running sales teams for companies that couldn't afford to get hiring, coaching, or closing wrong. I learned the hard way that most sales problems aren't sales problems. They're leadership problems wearing a sales team's uniform.

In 2015 I left my last director role to start Regina Lynn Leadership & Sales Consulting, working with owners and admissions directors who wanted their teams to sell with more confidence and less friction. Since then I've worked with career schools, home services companies, and B2B teams across the region, in rooms of three and rooms of thirty.

My approach is direct. I sit in on real calls, watch real presentations, and build the coaching plan around what's actually happening, not what the org chart says should be happening. Clients tell me the first session usually stings a little. The results are what keep them coming back.

“

Selling well isn't about talking someone into a yes. It's about getting clear enough, fast enough, that the right people can say yes to themselves.

Regina Lynn, Founder

01 / Background

Built in retail, admissions, and B2B

I've carried a quota in three different industries, which means I've made the mistakes most consultants only read about. That's why the coaching is specific: real scripts, real objection handling, real numbers, not general theory borrowed from a textbook.

02 / Philosophy

Accountability first, motivation second

Most sales training tries to fire people up. I'd rather build a system where showing up and doing the work is the expectation, not the exception. Motivation fades. Clear standards and honest feedback don't.
